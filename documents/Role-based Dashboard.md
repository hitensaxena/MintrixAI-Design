## Purpose

The **Role-Based Dashboard** is the passive observation Surface of Mintrix. 

If the `Approval Inbox` is for action and the `Daily Feed` is for linear triage, the Dashboard is for macroscopic analysis. It is primarily accessed by School Leadership (Principals, Directors, Head Admins).

---

## 1. Structural Philosophy

Mintrix Dashboards do not use infinite grids of raw data tables. They employ highly aggregated widget blocks that compress thousands of raw rows into single, actionable metrics using the "Editorial Intelligence" design language (stark contrasts, heavy typography).

*   **Widget-Based Layout**: The dashboard is composed of modular `<FeatureGrid>` elements.
*   **The "At a Glance" Rule**: Principals must be able to understand the entire operational health of the school within 3 seconds of looking at the Dashboard.

---

## 2. Core Dashboard Metrics

The widgets displayed are strictly gated entirely by the user's Autonomy ceiling.

<FeatureGrid>

</SurfaceCard>

</FeatureGrid>

---

## 3. The "Deep Dive" Transition

Dashboards are read-only surfaces. Users cannot fix underlying issues from the Dashboard itself.

Instead, the `<Widget>` acts as a hyperlink portal. When a Principal clicks the "12 infractions by Mr. Smith" warning on the Disciplinary Gravity Widget, the interface physically slides the dashboard out, transitioning the Principal instantly to a deep `Workspace Surface` pre-filtered to Mr. Smith's personnel and class records.

This ensures Dashboards do not become bloated with execution logic, maintaining absolute separation of concerns.
