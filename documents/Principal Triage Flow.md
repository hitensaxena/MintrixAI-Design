## Architectural Purpose

This document details the **Principal Triage Flow**, executed natively via the `Exception Center`.

While the `Approval Inbox` handles operations the AI *knows* how to execute but lacks the *authority* to do, the `Exception Center` handles catastrophic reality failures. These are scenarios where the AI *cannot physically solve the problem* due to conflicting variables, insufficient resources, or missing data. 

The system relies on human intuition to act as a forced override.

---

## 1. The Detection: Physical Reality vs Algorithmic Math

Mintrix operates the school as a mathematically balanced organism. When physical reality breaks the math, the system triggers a systemic rupture.

*   **The Conflict**: 4 Teachers call in sick at 7:45 AM due to a local flu outbreak. 
*   **The Algorithm Failure**: The `Operator Agent` attempts to dynamically reassign substitute teachers. However, `Available Substitutes = 2`. The math breaks. 14 class periods across the day are now "headless" (unstaffed), representing a severe legal and safety liability.

### UX State 1: The Rupture Signal
The system does not silently let the classes proceed, hoping someone figures it out. It generates a high-severity `Crimson Signal`.
*   **Visuals**: A massive `#dc2626` banner drops across the top of the Principal's `Role-based Dashboard`. The screen's baseline shifts from calm silver to urgent red tones. The UI literally throbs.

---

## 2. The Assessment: The Exception Center

The Principal clicks the Crimson Alarm and is pulled instantly into the `Exception Center`. 

**This is not an inbox; this is a war room.**

### The Macro-Event Aggregation (Anti-Fatigue)
A poorly designed CRM would generate 14 separate "Class Unstaffed" tickets, causing the Principal to panic and try to solve them 1 by 1. 

Mintrix explicitly forbids this. The AI's `Exception Funnel` analyzes the 14 tickets, realizes they share a root cause (The Flu), and aggregates them into a single massive **Macro-Event Card**: *"Systemic Staffing Deficit: 14 Sections Uncovered (Root: 4 Absences)."*

### UX State 2: Triage Options (The AI's Last Resort)
The AI cannot solve the problem autonomously without overstepping, but it *must* draft potential triage paths to save the human time. The Principal UI displays 3 actionable options, ranked explicitly by the level of institutional disruption:

1.  **Option A (Mild Disruption)**: "Merge Sections 4A and 4B into the Auditorium. Reassign Teacher Y from planning period to oversee the mass group."
2.  **Option B (Moderate Disruption)**: "Cancel non-core subjects (PE, Art) for the day. Reallocate PE/Art teachers to cover core math and science classes."
3.  **Option C (Severe Disruption)**: "Send all 14 headless sections to the cafeteria for continuous Study Hall, rotating available admins as supervisors."

The Principal can expand any of these options to see the **Blast Radius Viewer**. Expanding Option A shows: *"Warning: Overwrites Drama Club's booking of the Auditorium at 10 AM."*

---

## 3. Execution: The Global Override

The Principal reviews the Blast Radius of Option A and determines it is the least chaotic choice for the students' academic day.

### UX State 3: Forcing the Math
1.  **The Intervention**: The Principal clicks the `Global Override` checkmark on Option A. 
2.  **The Ripple Execution**: The moment the human applies their legal signature to the Override, the Agent regains its `Operator` status and violently restructures the timetable.
    *   It re-books the Auditorium, silently auto-canceling the Drama Club.
    *   It generates high-priority, vibrating Push Notifications for Teacher Y, alerting them to report to the Auditorium.
    *   It sends automated apologies to the Drama Club regarding the venue cancellation.
    *   It logs the entire emergency maneuver into the `Transparency Log` for the end-of-year audit.

The catastrophic staffing crisis is contained and algorithmically resolved in 4 human clicks.
