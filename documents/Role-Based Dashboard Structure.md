## Purpose

This document defines the structural composition of the **Role Dashboards** for each primary persona in Mintrix.

While all personas share the same "Surface Families" (Daily Feed, Approval Inbox, etc.), the **Dashboard Structure** determines how these surfaces are weighted, ordered, and populated to reflect each role's specific mission and time horizon.

---

## 1. Owner Dashboard Structure
*Focus: Institutional Health & Strategic Risk*

The Owner’s dashboard is a "High-Altitude" surface. It prioritizes cross-campus trends over day-to-day tasks.

| Component | Weight | Content Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Institutional Health (Role Dashboard)** | **Primary** | Multi-campus comparison (Fee health, Academic drift, Staff stability). |
| **Strategic Exceptions** | Secondary | High-severity reputational or financial risks that haven't been resolved by Principals. |
| **Transparency Log (High-Level)** | Tertiary | Summary of major autonomous actions taken across the portfolio. |
| **Daily Feed** | Minimal | Only ultra-high-priority briefings or scheduled strategic reviews. |

---

## 2. Principal Dashboard Structure
*Focus: Coherence, Continuity, & Risk Triage*

The Principal’s dashboard is the most complex. It balances "What is happening now" with "What is breaking."

| Component | Weight | Content Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Feed (The Pulse)** | **Primary** | Today’s schedule disruptions, morning briefing, and urgent highlights. |
| **Exception Center (The Triage)** | **Primary** | Academic drift (classes behind plan), attendance spikes, and unresolved event dependencies. |
| **Approval Inbox (The Judgment)** | Secondary | Sensitive parent comms, teacher intervention approvals, and event releases. |
| **School Health (Role Dashboard)** | Secondary | Snapshot of syllabus progress, fee collection, and staff attendance. |

---

## 3. Admin Dashboard Structure
*Focus: Completion, Accuracy, & Operational Flow*

The Admin’s dashboard is a "Workhorse" surface. It prioritizes task completion and system calibration.

| Component | Weight | Content Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Feed (Task-Based)** | **Primary** | Pending fee follow-ups, substitution requirements, and upcoming notice deadlines. |
| **Setup & Confidence Surface** | **Primary** | Missing foundation data, curriculum mapping gaps, and calibration progress. |
| **Approval Inbox (Operational)** | Secondary | Routine event approvals, ad-hoc payment releases, and notice circulars. |
| **Workspace Surface (Contextual)** | Secondary | Deep-dives into specific event planning or fee reconciliation. |

---

## 4. Teacher Dashboard Structure
*Focus: Preparedness, Empathy, & Academic Progress*

The Teacher’s dashboard is "Class-Centric." It prioritizes the immediate needs of their students and lessons.

| Component | Weight | Content Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Feed (The Classroom)** | **Primary** | Today’s timetable, student absences, and "Ready-to-Teach" lesson briefings. |
| **Class Dashboard (Role Dashboard)** | **Primary** | Syllabus pace vs. plan, student risk alerts, and recent assessment trends. |
| **Workspace Surface (Teaching)** | Secondary | Focused view for lesson execution, attendance marking, and parent messaging. |
| **Approval Inbox (Student-Level)** | Minimal | Individual student intervention sign-offs or trip consent tracking. |

---

## 5. Structural Layout Rules

To maintain a consistent "Operating System" feel, all role dashboards follow these layout rules:

1.  **The Left Rail (Identity & Navigation)**: Persistent access to the primary surfaces (Feed, Inbox, Exceptions) and the Persona-Agent status.
2.  **The Center Stage (The Mission)**: The "Primary Weight" component listed in the tables above occupies the largest screen real estate.
3.  **The Intelligence Sidebar (Context & Support)**: Contextual "Agent Nudges," Chat/Query access, and recent Transparency Log snippets.
4.  **The "Step-Down" Indicator**: Every component must visually indicate its **Confidence Level** (High, Medium, Low) and allow the user to "View Why."

---

## 6. Dashboard State Changes

Dashboards are not static; they adapt based on the **School Lifecycle**:
-   **Calibration Phase (Week 1-4)**: Admin and Principal dashboards prioritize the "Setup & Confidence" surface. Teacher dashboards act primarily as "Assistants."
-   **Steady State**: Dashboards shift focus to the "Daily Feed" and "Exception Center" as the system moves toward "Operator" mode.
-   **Exam/Event Peak**: Dashboards dynamically elevate the "Event Workspace" or "Assessment Triage" to the primary position.
