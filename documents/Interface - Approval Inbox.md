## Purpose

The Approval Inbox is the system's formal judgment surface. It collects prepared actions that the system has developed but should not execute without explicit human authorization.

It is designed to turn the human into a "Judge" rather than a "Writer" or "Task Manager."

---

## 1. Surface Characteristics

-   **Decision-First**: Every item in the inbox must have a clear "Approve," "Edit," or "Reject" action ladder.
-   **High-Context**: The inbox provides all the data signals (Student history, policy, rationale) needed to make a decision without leaving the surface.
-   **Low-Noise**: Only high-sensitivity or context-rich actions are routed here.
-   **Clearly Reversible**: Actions taken from the inbox should be auditable and, where possible, reversible from the Transparency Log.

---

## 2. Typical Item Types

-   **Sensitive Communication**: Parent message drafts for student incidents or academic risk.
-   **Workflow Sign-offs**: Intervention plans, syllabus recovery paths, or event participant rosters.
-   **Policy Exceptions**: Approving a fee waiver or a late event registration.
-   **Staffing Decisions**: Final sign-off on a suggested substitution plan.

---

## 3. The "Judgment Card" Structure

Every item in the Approval Inbox is presented as a standardized **Judgment Card**:

1.  **The Proposal**: What is the system suggesting? (e.g., *"Send 'Attendance Alert' message to Parent A"*).
2.  **The Trigger**: Why is it suggesting this? (e.g., *"Student A has been absent for 3 days without a note"*).
3.  **The Evidence**: Supporting data (e.g., *"Previous pattern: Absent every second Tuesday"*).
4.  **The Impact**: What happens after approval? (*"Message sent to Parent via WhatsApp; logged in student history"*).

---

## 4. Persona-Specific Inbox Use

-   **Teacher**: Approving student-level intervention drafts or parent notifications.
-   **Principal**: High-level staff substitutions, disciplinary escalations, or campus-wide notices.
-   **Admin**: Event releases, fee reminders for high-value accounts, or circular distribution.

---

## 5. Design Rule: "Confidence and Authority"

The Inbox must visually communicate the system's **Confidence Level**. If confidence is low, the Inbox should explicitly ask for more manual editing or a policy override.
