## Purpose

The **Workspace** is the heavy execution Surface in Mintrix. 

When a user needs to definitively alter the `Setup Architecture` (e.g., building a timetable) or the `Living Curriculum State` (e.g., entering grades, writing a lesson plan), they transition into a Workspace.

---

## 1. Structural Philosophy

Workspaces are structurally heavy and dense. They break from the ambient, simple `FeatureCards` of the Feed and rely on robust input tables, split-pane forms, and persistent sidebars.

*   **Focus State**: Entering a Workspace often minimizes or dims the primary navigation. It demands full mechanical attention from the human.
*   **The Copilot Rail**: The right-hand side of every execution Workspace is permanently occupied by the `Intelligence Sidebar`—the physical manifestation of the user's `Persona Agent`.

---

## 2. The Execution Loop

A standard Workspace flow is a conversation between the Human laying the foundation and the AI calculating the consequences.

## 3. Typology of Workspaces

Different roles require entirely different Workspace schemas.

*   **The Teacher Gradebook (`Assistant` Mode)**: A dense tabular array where Teachers input marks. The Intelligence Sidebar quietly predicts pass/fail ratios based on historical data.
*   **The Setup Workspace (`Tool` Mode)**: The core Admin surface defining Venues, Subjects, and Periods. It relies on massive, bulk-upload capable data grids and hard validation rules.
*   **The Mass Communication Spawner (`Operator` Mode)**: Where mass templates (e.g., fee reminders) are drafted before being released to automatically generate `Action Cards` in Parent Daily Feeds.
