## Purpose

This document outlines the **Teacher Daily Teaching** workflow.

The core intent of the Teacher Workspace is to ruthlessly eliminate the administration of pedagogy, leaving only human instruction. The Teacher interacts almost exclusively with the `Living Curriculum State` and the `Teacher Agent`.

---

## 1. The Morning Initialization (Awareness Loop)

Before period 1 begins, the Teacher receives a highly compressed brief in their `Daily Feed`.

### The "Today" Array
*   **The Roster**: "Class 10 Math: 40 Students."
*   **Intelligence Injections**:
    *   *Red Card*: "John is absent today (Reported by Admin)." 
    *   *Amber Card*: "Sarah and Michael failed yesterday's pop quiz. Consider targeting them for Questions 2 and 4."
    *   *Green Card*: "Syllabus on track. Topic: Advanced Geometry."

This prevents the Teacher from wasting 10 minutes performing manual roll calls or checking previous gradebooks.

---

## 2. In-Class Execution (The Workspace Loop)

During or immediately following the period, the Teacher opens the **Class Workspace**.

<SurfaceCard title="Action 1: Logging Reality">
The primary physical action of the Workspace. The Teacher must confirm:
1. Did I teach what the system predicted?
2. Did any student behave anomalously?
If the Teacher clicks "Yes", the AI logs the syllabus completion.

<SurfaceCard title="Action 2: The Assessment Spawner">

The **Teacher Agent** intercepts it, cross-references historical pacing, and drafts a digital assignment card pushing instantly to the Students' apps.
</SurfaceCard>

<SurfaceCard title="Action 3: The Intervention Trigger">
The Teacher notices a student struggling. They type a note. The `Semantic Processor` parses it. If severe, it bypasses the `Teacher Agent` and routes a `Judgment Card` to the `Principal Agent` recommending parent escalation.
</SurfaceCard>

## 4. Edge Cases and Friction Handling

Because teaching is a highly volatile environment, the system must degrade safely when standard expected routines break.

*   **Network Failure/Offline Mode**: If the Teacher's device loses connectivity, the system does not block teaching. The Teacher can manually dismiss the AI recommendations and proceed. Once reconnected, the `Living Curriculum Engine` silently back-calculates the delta without punishing the Teacher with 50 delayed notifications.
*   **The Substitute Teacher Injection**: If a Teacher calls out sick 15 minutes before the period begins, the system instantly halts the standard Teacher Workflow for that class. It automatically generates a compressed version of the `Daily Feed` for the assigned Substitute Teacher, focusing *only* on the syllabus location and any immediate safety alerts (e.g., student allergies), bypassing the standard longitudinal data a permanent Teacher would see.

---

## 3. The End-of-Day Aggregation

As the Teacher leaves, they do not manually compile a "Daily Report."

1.  **System Sweep**: The autonomous engines compile all 5 periods taught by the Teacher.
2.  **Syllabus Delta Calculation**: It calculates if the Teacher is falling behind globally across all their subjects.
3.  **The Tomorrow Prediction**: The `Teacher Agent` automatically drafts tomorrow's `Today Array`, staging the next syllabus nodes and any pending homework review tasks into the Feed, closing the 24-hour loop.
